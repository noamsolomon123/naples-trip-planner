# Naples Region Budget — Couple, 17–24 Oct 2026 (7 nights)

**Who:** Couple, both ~19, NOT EU citizens (matters for discounts — see below).
**Bases:** Naples (3 nights) → Sorrento area (2 nights) → Salerno/Amalfi side (2 nights). No car.
**Target:** €60–90/day/person, **excluding flights**.
**Prices as of:** July 2026 research (2025/26 season). Expect ±10% drift; October is shoulder season (Amalfi ferry/bus schedules thin out late October — most still run through Oct).

---

## 1. Key Fact: The 18–25 Discount Does NOT Apply to You

Italian state sites (Pompeii, Herculaneum, Castel Sant'Elmo, MANN) sell a **€2 reduced ticket for ages 18–25 — EU citizens only** (plus holders of Italian residence permits / reciprocal-agreement states; Israel is not on the standard list). **Budget full price.** Worst case you paid correctly; if a ticket clerk accepts your passport anyway, you save ~€30 — treat it as a bonus, don't plan on it.

Free first Sunday of the month (Domenica al Museo) = **4 Oct 2026 — before your trip. Doesn't help you.**

### Verdict on €60–90/day/pp
- **€60/day is possible but strict:** B&B/hostel privates, Vico Equense instead of Sorrento proper, Procida instead of Capri, street food as the default. Zero slack.
- **€75–85/day is the honest sweet spot** — everything on your wishlist except the Blue Grotto splurge.
- **€90/day = genuinely comfortable**, with Capri + Blue Grotto + romantic dinners. Note: a real "splurge" version drifts to ~€110-115/pp/day, slightly above your band.
- **Realistic trip total for the couple: €1,000–1,450** (most likely ~€1,250).

## 8. Money-Saving Tips

1. **Artecard — mostly NOT worth it for you.** The youth 3-day (€30) is **EU-only**, like the €2 tickets. Adult Campania 3-day = €41/pp, includes Pompeii + 1 more site free + Circumvesuviana transport. You'd pay €82/couple to cover ~€75–85 of value — roughly break-even, with a clunky app. **Skip it; buy tickets individually.** Only reconsider the **365 Lite (€26/pp)** if it still covers Pompeii + Herculaneum entry at your travel date — then it's a real ~€10–15/pp save; verify at campaniartecard.it.
2. **Castel Sant'Elmo on a Tuesday** = €2.50 instead of €5.
3. **Castel dell'Ovo is free** — best sunset view in Naples for €0.
4. **Procida over Capri saves ~€90–110/couple** (ferry €25–30 rt vs €90+, food half price, arguably more romantic and zero crowds).
5. **Ferry vs bus on Amalfi coast:** SITA 24h pass (€10) is cheapest; ferries cost ~3x but save you the nausea + 90-min queues — do bus one way, ferry back (Amalfi→Salerno €10–12).
6. **Sleep in Salerno, not Amalfi/Positano** — same coast, half the room price, Travelmar ferries at the door.
7. **Vesuvius: book the official €10–12 ticket yourself** + Busvia/EAV shuttle, instead of €50+/pp "tours".
8. **Lunch = street food, always.** €10/couple lunches in Naples are real. Save sit-downs for dinner.
9. **House wine (vino della casa)** €4–8/half-litre vs €25+ bottles.
10. **Book Pompeii + Vesuvius online 1–2 weeks ahead** — Vesuvius sells out, no gate sales.

## 9. Payments & Cash

- Cards/Apple Pay accepted nearly everywhere incl. Circumvesuviana machines and SITA app; **carry €50–80 cash** for street food stalls, small B&B city tax, Blue Grotto rowers.
- Use no-FX-fee card; always choose to be charged **in EUR**, never in your home currency (DCC rip-off).
- Avoid airport exchange desks; Bancomat ATMs of real banks (Intesa, BPER) over Euronet.
