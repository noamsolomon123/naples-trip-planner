# Naples–Sorrento–Salerno: Transportation Guide (17–24 Oct 2026, no car)

**Trip frame:** Land NAP 17 Oct → depart NAP 24 Oct. 7 nights, public transport only.
**Recommended base split:** **Naples 3 nights (17–20) → Sorrento 2 nights (20–22) → Salerno 2 nights (22–24)** — ends 35–40 min by train from Naples, the safest possible flight-day logistics. (Why Salerno and not Amalfi as base 3: see bottom.)

---

## 1. Arrival: NAP Airport → Naples city

| Option | Price | Time | Frequency | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Alibus (ANM)** ✅ | **€5** | ~15 min to Garibaldi, ~35 min to port | Every 15–30 min, ~06:00–24:00 | Stops: Piazza Garibaldi (Napoli Centrale) → Molo Beverello/port. Buy at vending machines in arrivals, online (QR ok), or ANM app |
| Metro Line 1 (Capodichino station) | ~€1.30–1.50 | ~20 min | frequent | Airport metro station opened; check it's running your dates — Alibus is the safe default |
| Taxi (fixed fare) | ~€18–25 to center | 15–25 min | — | Insist on *tariffa predeterminata* (fixed fare list) |

**Skip-Naples option (not needed for our plan):** Curreri Viaggi direct bus NAP airport → Sorrento, **€10–13**, ~75–80 min, ~8–10 departures/day, luggage included. Useful to know for a rainy-day plan B. Book at curreriviaggi.it or pay driver cash.

## 2. Naples ↔ Pompeii ↔ Sorrento: Circumvesuviana (EAV)

- Departs **Napoli Porta Nolana / Napoli Garibaldi** (lower level of Centrale station).
- **Prices:** Naples→Pompei Scavi **~€2.60–2.80**; Naples→Sorrento **~€4**; Sorrento→Pompei **~€2.60**.
- **Frequency:** roughly every 30 min, ~05:00–21:30. Full run Naples–Sorrento ~70 min.
- **Reality check:** commuter train — old, crowded, graffiti, **no luggage racks** (bags on lap/between legs), and **pickpocketing is common**, worst on the crowded urban stretch Garibaldi→Ercolano. Wear bags in front, phones in front pockets, nothing in back pockets. It's fine — thousands of tourists daily — just be switched on.
- **Move day 20 Oct (Naples→Sorrento with bags):** board at Porta Nolana (the origin station, one stop before Garibaldi) to grab seats; travel mid-morning off-peak.

### Campania Express (tourist version, same tracks)
- Guaranteed seat, A/C, luggage space, far fewer pickpockets. **~€12–15 one way**, 4–6 departures/day.
- ⚠️ **Seasonal: runs roughly mid-March to mid/late October.** For 17–24 Oct 2026 it may be in its final days or already finished — **check eavsrl.it / campaniaexpress.com** close to the trip. If running, worth the €8 upgrade for the move day with luggage.

## 3. Sorrento → Positano → Amalfi: SITA Sud bus

- **The** year-round workhorse; runs daily including October (autumn timetable, slightly fewer runs than summer — typically 1–2/hour on Sorrento–Amalfi).
- **Fares (Unico Costiera zones):** Sorrento→Positano **€2.60**, Positano→Praiano **€1.80**, Sorrento→Amalfi ~**€3.40**. **COSTIERASITA 24h pass: €10** unlimited — buy this on your Positano+Amalfi day, pays off in 3 rides.
- **Buy tickets BEFORE boarding** — tabaccheria, newsstands, bars; in Sorrento at the booth by the Circumvesuviana station. Cannot buy on board. Validate on the machine when boarding.
- Sorrento→Positano ~50 min, →Amalfi ~100 min. Sit on the **right side (sea side)** heading toward Amalfi. October = much less summer gridlock, but afternoon Positano→Sorrento buses can still fill; board at the first stop (Sponda or Chiesa Nuova early).
- Timetables: sitasudtrasporti.it (official), positano.com/en/bus-schedule (readable).

## 4. Ferries — CRITICAL October status

**Good news: 17–24 Oct 2026 is INSIDE ferry season.** Amalfi Coast ferry season runs late March → **late October (companies typically stop ~25–31 Oct)**. Your dates get the reduced autumn timetable (fewer daily runs), not a shutdown. After ~1 Nov it drops to little/nothing until spring.

| Route | Operator | ~Price | ~Time | Oct status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sorrento → Positano / Amalfi | Alilauro Gruson, NLG, Coastlines | €17–20 | 30–50 min | Running, reduced schedule; confirm on Ferryhopper |
| **Salerno ↔ Amalfi** | **Travelmar** | ~€10–12 | ~40 min | Running (Travelmar is the most reliable late-season operator) |
| **Salerno ↔ Positano** | Travelmar | ~€14–16 | ~75 min | Running, fewer sailings |
| Amalfi ↔ Positano | Travelmar/Coastlines | ~€10 | ~25 min | Running |

- Book/check on **travelmar.it**, **nlg.it**, or aggregate via **ferryhopper.com**.
- Travelmar luggage: 1 hand bag (45×35×20) free, small fee for a suitcase.
- **Weather caveat:** October sea can cancel sailings day-of. Always have the SITA bus (or Salerno train) as plan B — never ferry-only on a day you must reach the airport.

## 5. Base-to-base move days

**20 Oct — Naples → Sorrento:** Circumvesuviana (or Campania Express if still running) from Porta Nolana, ~70 min, €4. Optional: stop at Pompei Scavi en route — station has a paid left-luggage / Bounce points by the entrance, and the site itself has free luggage deposit (small bags).

**22 Oct — Sorrento → Salerno (the scenic move):**
- Best: SITA bus Sorrento→Amalfi (~100 min), store bags in Amalfi (deposit near the port, ~€5–8/bag), enjoy Amalfi + quick bus/ferry hop to Ravello or Atrani, then **Travelmar ferry Amalfi→Salerno (~40 min, ~€10)** in the late afternoon. Rough-sea fallback: SITA bus Amalfi→Salerno (~75 min, runs year-round).
- Lazy option: ferry Sorrento→Amalfi direct (~€17–20) if the schedule fits.

**24 Oct — Salerno → NAP airport (flight day):**
- **Train Salerno → Napoli Centrale: ~50 trains/day.** Regionale ~**€5.60–6.60**, 35–45 min; Frecciarossa/Italo ~€10–17, 23–36 min. Then **Alibus €5** Garibaldi→airport (15 min) or taxi ~€20. Total door-to-door under 1.5 h — this is why Salerno is the last base.

## 6. Tickets & apps

- **Unico Campania app** (iOS/Android) — buys EAV (Circumvesuviana) and SITA Sud tickets on the phone. Create account in advance (SMS activation can be flaky); Visa/MC/PayPal accepted. Paper from tabaccherie always works as backup.
- **Unico Campania 3T**: €20, 3 days, ALL regional transport incl. Circumvesuviana + SITA Amalfi buses — do the math for your Sorrento block (2 coast days ≈ €10 pass + €10 pass anyway, so only worth it if combining train legs).
- **Campania ArteCard** (campaniartecard.it): 3-day €41 version = transport + 2 free major sites (Pompeii €19 alone!) + discounts. **Strong candidate for the Naples block** if visiting Pompeii + Archaeological Museum.
- Trenitalia app / Italo app for the Salerno–Naples leg (Regionale tickets are fixed-price, no need to prebook).
- Ferries: Ferryhopper app or operator sites.

## 7. Luggage storage

- **Napoli Centrale:** official KiPoint deposit inside station (~€6/bag/day) + **Bounce / Radical / Stasher / Nannybag** networks from **€1.50–5/bag/day** all around Garibaldi and centro storico.
- **Sorrento:** deposits near the Circumvesuviana station (~€5–8/day), plus Bounce partners.
- **Pompei Scavi:** free deposit inside the site for small bags; paid deposits by the station for suitcases.
- **Amalfi:** paid deposit near the bus/ferry terminal (~€5–8/bag) — enables the "sightsee mid-move" trick on 22 Oct.
- **Salerno:** Bounce/Radical points near station.

## Why Salerno (not Amalfi town) as base 3

1. **Flight-day safety:** Salerno→Naples = 40-min train, ~50/day, weatherproof. Amalfi→Naples = bus+train or ferry, 2.5–3 h, weather-exposed.
2. **Price:** Salerno doubles €50–70 vs Amalfi €90–130 for the same quality.
3. **Access unchanged:** Travelmar ferries from Salerno reach Amalfi (40 min) and Positano (75 min) all through your dates — Salerno is a fine day-trip launchpad, plus it has a real (cheap, good) food scene of its own.
