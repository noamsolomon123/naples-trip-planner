**Trip: 17–24 October 2026 · Young couple from Israel · Public transport only · Bases: Naples → Sorrento → Amalfi/Salerno**

## 1. Safety First (Read Before Arrival)

### Overall verdict
Naples is **safer than its reputation** — Campania logs ~3 pickpocket victims per 1,000 residents, well below Rome (14/1,000) and Milan (7/1,000). Violent crime against tourists is rare. The real risks are **petty theft and scams**, concentrated exactly where you'll be: Garibaldi station, the Circumvesuviana, and crowded old-town alleys. Since 2024–2025 a plain-clothes police unit ("Polmetro") patrols Naples metro trains and platforms, which has helped.

### Pickpocket hotspots (your specific routes)
| Where | Risk | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| **Circumvesuviana train** (Naples–Pompei–Sorrento) | HIGH | Most notorious line in Italy for pickpockets. Crowded, old trains, no AC crush at doors. Thieves work in teams near doors, strike as train stops. |
| **Napoli Centrale / Piazza Garibaldi** | HIGH | Crowded platforms + underground Circumvesuviana level. Distraction teams, "helpful" strangers at ticket machines. Square itself gets sketchy after dark. |
| **Via Toledo / Spaccanapoli / centro storico** | MEDIUM | Crowd-crush pickpocketing; costumed "gladiator" photo hustlers demand money after selfies. |
| **Quartieri Spagnoli (Spanish Quarter) at night** | MEDIUM | By day fine and atmospheric. At night stick to the main lit lanes (around Via Toledo side); avoid empty upper alleys late. Not a no-go zone, just be alert. |
| **Buses & funiculars in Naples** | MEDIUM | Same crowd rules as metro. |

**Known Circumvesuviana scam:** a "couple with stroller" blocks the door as you exit and asks help lifting the stroller — while you bend to help, the partner behind you picks your pocket. Decline politely, keep moving.

### Scooter bag-snatching (scippo)
Classic Naples crime: two riders on a scooter snatch a bag/phone from pedestrians near the curb, or a phone held out for photos/maps.
- Walk with bags on the **building side**, not the street side.
- **Cross-body bag, zipped, in front** — never a shoulder-dangling bag.
- Don't stand at the curb staring at your phone; step into a doorway to check maps.
- Don't leave phones on café tables facing the street.

### Common scams — how they actually work
1. **Taxi overcharge (esp. airport)**: Naples has FIXED fares (tariffa predeterminata) — airport↔Centro/port is a fixed price (~€18–25 depending on zone; posted on a card in every licensed taxi). Drivers "forget" and quote €40+. Say **"tariffa fissa, per favore"** before departing, or use the Alibus (€5). Only white licensed taxis from official ranks.
2. **Restaurant tricks**: `coperto` (cover charge, €1–3/person) is LEGAL and normal **if printed on the menu**. Scam = coperto not on menu, "servizio" 15–20% added on top, or waiter bringing unordered antipasti/water and charging. Refuse anything you didn't order; always see a printed menu with prices before sitting. Check the itemized bill (conto).
3. **Fake/overpriced tickets**: buy transport tickets ONLY from official machines, tabacchi (tobacco shops), or contactless Tap&Go — never from people "helping" at machines in Garibaldi. Ticket clerks at Garibaldi were literally suspended in 2025 for shortchanging tourists — **count your change**.
4. **Validation fine trap**: paper tickets must be validated (stamped) before boarding; on Tap&Go you must **tap out** too (distance fares) or you get fined.
5. **Friendship bracelet / rose / "free" gift**: someone ties a bracelet on your wrist or hands your girlfriend a rose, then demands money. Keep hands in pockets, firm "No, grazie", walk on.
6. **Fake petition / clipboard**: distraction while accomplice picks pockets.
7. **"Closed" hotel / helpful local rerouting you**: ignore, verify yourself.

### If something happens
- **Emergency (all): 112** (EU-wide, English available). Police 113, ambulance 118, fire 115.
- Robbed: file a report (denuncia) at any Carabinieri/Polizia station — needed for insurance.
- Lost Israeli passport: **Embassy of Israel, Rome** — Via Michele Mercati 14, +39 06 3619 8500. File police report first, then contact embassy for emergency travel document.
- Card stolen: block immediately via bank app; keep a backup card in the hotel safe.

### Golden rules for you two
- Split cash/cards between you; never all money in one bag.
- Phone leash or deep front pocket on the Circumvesuviana.
- Ride nearer the front car / near staff; stand away from doors with bags in front.
- Nothing valuable in back pockets or backpack outer pockets. Backpack worn front in crowds.
- At night: Garibaldi area, Museo area side streets, and empty Quartieri Spagnoli alleys — take a taxi/metro rather than walking.

## 2. October Weather & Packing

### The numbers (Naples/Sorrento, mid-late October)
- **Daytime highs:** 21–24°C (70–75°F). **Nights:** 13–16°C (55–61°F). Noticeable day/night gap — evenings feel cool.
- **Rain:** October is the start of the wet season — roughly **6–9 rain days** in the month, ~100–130 mm total. Early October drier; **your window (17–24) has real chance of 1–3 wet days**, often short heavy showers rather than all-day rain.
- **Sea temperature:** ~21–22°C (70–72°F). **Swimming is realistic on sunny days** if you're not cold-sensitive — many people still swim mid-October. Beach clubs mostly close ~Oct 15, so it's free-beach swimming (Marina Grande Sorrento, Amalfi/Salerno beaches). Bring swimwear, don't build the trip around it.
- **Sunshine:** ~6 hours/day, more sunny days than grey ones.
- **Sunset:** ~18:20 on Oct 17 → ~18:10 on Oct 24 (clocks are still on summer time — DST ends Oct 25, the day after you leave). Plan day trips to finish by ~18:00; dark by 18:45.
- **Vesuvius summit:** ~10°C colder + windy at the crater (1,281 m). Can be cloud-capped.

### Verdict
**Excellent sightseeing weather** — warm days, no summer crowds/heat, Pompeii and hiking are pleasant. Pack for sun AND a couple of rainy, cool evenings. Swimming = bonus, not guarantee.

### Packing list (October, this trip)
**Clothing**
- [ ] 3–4 t-shirts / light tops + 1–2 long-sleeve shirts
- [ ] 1 warm layer each (fleece/sweater) — evenings + Vesuvius
- [ ] Light waterproof jacket or packable rain shell (essential)
- [ ] Jeans/long trousers + 1 pair shorts (warm days)
- [ ] Swimwear + quick-dry towel (chance of a swim)
- [ ] Comfortable broken-in walking shoes with grip — Pompeii stones, Amalfi stairs, Vesuvius gravel are brutal on bad shoes
- [ ] Scarf/shawl (her) + something covering shoulders/knees — for churches (see dress code) and cool evenings

**Gear**
- [ ] Cross-body anti-theft bag (zips, worn front) — most important item
- [ ] Small daypack for day trips
- [ ] Compact umbrella
- [ ] Refillable water bottle (fountains + tap water fine)
- [ ] Power bank (long day trips, navigation drains battery)
- [ ] EU plug adapter — Italy: **Type C/F/L, 230V** (Israeli Type C/H plugs: standard 2-pin Type C chargers fit; grounded Type H does NOT — bring adapter)
- [ ] Sunscreen + sunglasses + cap (midday sun still strong)
- [ ] Small first-aid: plasters (blisters!), painkillers, motion-sickness pills (Capri ferry can be rough in October)

**Documents**
- [ ] Passports (valid 3+ months beyond departure) + photocopies/photos stored separately
- [ ] Travel insurance policy (get one — Israeli HMO doesn't cover abroad)
- [ ] Booked tickets offline (Vesuvius, Pompeii) — signal drops in crowds

**Don't bother bringing:** heavy coat, formal shoes, big beach gear, drone (restricted over Pompeii/archaeological sites).

**Israeli entry note:** Israeli passport = visa-free Schengen (90 days). The EU **EES biometric system** (fingerprints/photo at first entry) rolled out from Oct 2025 — expect a slightly longer first passport control. Check ETIAS status before travel (was expected to launch late 2026; if live, it's a cheap online pre-authorization).

## 3. Cultural Cheat Sheet

### Meal times (Naples runs late)
- **Breakfast:** bar culture — cornetto + espresso standing at the counter, ~€2.50. Cappuccino is a morning drink; after ~11:00 locals switch to espresso (nobody will stop you, but you'll be marked as tourist).
- **Lunch:** 12:30–14:30. Kitchens genuinely close ~14:30–15:00.
- **Dinner:** restaurants open **19:00–19:30 at the earliest**, locals eat 20:00–21:30. A place serving dinner at 18:00 is a tourist trap by definition.
- Pizza al portafoglio (folded street pizza, €2–3) and friggitoria fried snacks fill any gap hours.

### Riposo & closures
- Many shops close **~13:00–16:00** (longer in the south). Plan shopping/errands for morning or evening.
- **Sunday:** many shops and some restaurants closed; churches busy with mass in the morning (visit as tourist outside mass times). Museums usually OPEN Sunday but many close **Monday** instead. Pompeii is open daily; check your specific museums.
- Many restaurants take one weekly rest day (giorno di riposo) — check Google Maps hours before crossing town.

### Tipping (relax, it's not the US/Israel)
- **Not expected.** Coperto/servizio on the bill replaces it.
- Great service: round up or leave €1–5 **in cash** (tips can't go on card).
- Taxis: round up to the nearest euro. Hotels: €1–2/bag if porter. No tipping at the bar counter.

### Church dress code
- **Shoulders and knees covered** (both of you) — enforced at Naples Duomo, Gesù Nuovo, Santa Chiara, Amalfi Cathedral, Positano church. Scarf in the daypack solves it. No hats inside, silence, no flash; some crypts ban photos entirely.

## 4. Practical Essentials

### SIM / eSIM
- **Easiest: eSIM before you fly.** Well-reviewed for Italy 2026: **Saily**, **Airalo**, **Holafly** (unlimited, ~€36.90/10 days), **Ubigi**, **Jetpac**. A 10-day / 10–20GB regional plan ≈ €10–20. Buy + install at home, activate on landing.
- Physical SIM: **Iliad** (~€10/month, huge data) or WindTre/TIM tourist SIMs at official stores (passport required). Skip airport kiosk markups.
- Coverage is good along your whole route incl. Capri/Amalfi; expect dead zones inside the Circumvesuviana tunnels and on trails.
- **Maps:** Google Maps works well (incl. transit for Naples metro/buses). Download **offline maps** for Naples + Sorrento + Amalfi coast. Circumvesuviana times: use the EAV site/app or Google, but verify on station boards — delays are chronic.

### Water & pharmacies
- **Tap water is safe** in Naples/Sorrento/Amalfi (EU standards, tested). Locals often buy bottled by habit/taste — you don't need to. Refill at fountains.
- **Pharmacies (Farmacia, green cross):** typically 08:30–13:00 & 15:30–19:30, Sat morning only, Sun closed — but a rotating **farmacia di turno** is always open (list posted on every pharmacy door / farmaciediturno.org). Pharmacists handle minor issues and speak decent English in tourist areas. Naples Centrale station has a long-hours pharmacy.

### Emergency numbers
- **112 — universal EU emergency** (police/medical/fire, English OK)
- 113 police · 118 ambulance · 115 fire · 1530 coast guard
- Israeli Embassy Rome: +39 06 3619 8500

### Sunday / holiday transit
- **Circumvesuviana runs a reduced Sunday/holiday timetable** — fewer trains, bigger gaps (can be 1/hour on some branches). Check the festivi column, plan Pompeii/return with buffer.
- SITA buses on the Amalfi coast also run reduced Sunday service.
- No public holidays fall in 17–24 Oct 2026 (next is Nov 1, after you leave).
- 17 Oct 2026 is a Saturday, so **Sunday 18 Oct** is your one reduced-service day. Make it a walking/Naples day or pre-check timetables.

### Strikes (sciopero) — a real Italian thing
- Transport strikes are announced **in advance** and usually last 24h with **guaranteed service windows** (roughly 06:00–09:00 and 18:00–21:00 for local transport, Mon–Sat).
- The Circumvesuviana operator (**EAV**) strikes several times a year (e.g., a 24h strike hit the line in May 2026); a reduced Naples–Pompei–Sorrento timetable is usually maintained.
- **How to check:** official list at the Ministry site **scioperi.mit.gov.it** (Commissione di Garanzia Sciopero), plus **striketracker.app/strikes-in-italy** in English, EAV website/Twitter, and Trenitalia strike page. Check once ~1 week before and the night before each intercity move.
- If a strike hits your Capri day: ferries strike separately/rarely — pivot day-trip order.

### Vesuvius in October — key facts
- **Open in October, hours 09:00–16:00** (last entry earlier; gates close to ascent ~1.5h before closing).
- **Online booking MANDATORY** — no tickets at the gate. Official channel: Vesuvius National Park / vivaticket. ~€12/adult, timed slots (~50 people per 10 min), released ~30 days ahead — **book 2–3+ days early**, arrive within 30 min of your slot or lose it.
- Trail No. 5 "Gran Cono": ~30–40 min steady uphill on volcanic gravel, moderate; grippy shoes + windproof layer + water.
- **Weather closure risk is real in October** — strong wind/rain suspends access without much notice. Book for a clear-forecast day, keep it swappable with Pompeii.
- Getting there car-free: EAV bus or Vesuvio Express from Ercolano Scavi (Circumvesuviana), or Busvia/park shuttles from Pompei — buy round trip.

### Money & misc
- Cards accepted nearly everywhere by law, but **carry €50–100 cash** — small pizzerias, market stalls, tabacchi, coin tips. Use bank ATMs (Intesa, UniCredit, BPER) attached to branches; **avoid Euronet ATMs** (bad rates). Always choose "charge in EUR", never the ATM's conversion.
- Timezone: Italy is 1 hour behind Israel (both on summer time during your trip).
- Photography: fine everywhere public; some church interiors/crypts ban it; ask before photographing market vendors' displays.

## 5. Quick Risk Summary

1. **Circumvesuviana + Garibaldi pickpockets** — your #1 realistic risk; front-worn zipped bag, phone away near doors.
2. **Scooter bag/phone snatching in Naples streets** — walk building-side, phone in doorways only.
3. **Taxi & restaurant overcharging** — fixed taxi fares, menu with printed prices, itemized bill.
4. **Strike/Sunday transit gaps** — check scioperi.mit.gov.it + EAV before every travel day; Sunday = reduced trains.
5. **Vesuvius sells out + weather closures** — book online days ahead, keep the day swappable.
